About Me

I am a spatial data scientist and landscape ecologist with 5 years of experience working with GIS and remote sensing tools. I recently earned an M.S. in Ecology, Evolution, and Conservation Biology from the University of Nevada, Reno. My interest in ecology is inspired by a love of the outdoors. For fun, I enjoy skiing, climbing, backpacking, caving, running, and more, but my favorite activity is ski mountaineering. This year, I summited Whitney (pictured left), Shasta, and Rainier.



Career Summary

I earned my B.S. in Environmental Science from the University of Washington which is where I first learned about GIS. After taking a class about spatial analysis in forest resources, I immediately signed up as a GIS Lab Assistant to teach other students how to find spatial data and take advantage of this incredible technology. I worked a number of seasonal jobs that gave me experience with field research in the subject matters of plant-insect interactions, fisheries, disease vector control, and riparian conservation. While I enjoyed my experiences in the field, I knew that I eventually wanted to work in data analytics, so I went back to school to learn technical data science skills such as programming, machine learning, spatial statistics, ecological forecasting, and more (as shown by my transcript). My master's thesis (manuscript in preparation for submission to peer-reviewed journal) examines spatial and temporal variations in ecosystem resistance to seasonal drought. Now, I'm pursuing a career that will allow me to build upon my analytical skills while working with complex ecological data.
