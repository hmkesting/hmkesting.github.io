About Me

Please see my resume for a summary of my work experience and specific software proficiencies.

I am a spatial data scientist and landscape ecologist with 5 years of experience working with GIS and remote sensing tools. I recently earned an M.S. in Ecology, Evolution, and Conservation Biology from the University of Nevada, Reno. Now I'm pursuing a career that will allow me to build upon my analytical skills while working with complex ecological data. My strengths include:

  • Programming in Python and R: I develop workflows for data cleaning, processing, and analysis. I also generate customizable figures and statistical analyses beyond prepackaged code.
  • Spatial Statistics and Modelling: I conduct variogram analysis and use spatial interpolation methods such as IDW, spline, and kriging. After developing a model, I analyze the spatial and frequency distributions of the residuals to inform decisions about model types, variables, and hyperparameter settings.
  • Remote Sensing and Imagery Analysis I use imagery at a variety of spatial resolutions to classify land cover types and detect changes in vegetation. Using structure from motion photogrammetry, I generate point clouds to analyze vegetation structure.
  • Technical Writing I think critically about the language I use to efficiently communicate scientific methods and concepts to people from a variety of backgrounds.
My interest in ecology is inspired by a love of the outdoors. For fun, I enjoy skiing, climbing, backpacking, caving, running, and more, but my favorite activity is ski mountaineering. This year, I summited Whitney (pictured left), Shasta, and Rainier.

Career Summary

I earned my B.S. in Environmental Science and Terrestrial Resource Management from the University of Washington which is where I first learned about GIS. I took a class about spatial analysis in forest resources and afterwards, I immediately signed up as a GIS Lab Assistant to teach other students how to find spatial data and take advantage of this incredible technology. For my thesis, I used ArcGIS to classify aerial imagery by land cover type to assess habitat suitability for a native bee species. I worked a number of seasonal jobs that gave me experience with field research in the subject matters of plant-insect interactions, fisheries, disease vector control, and riparian conservation. While I enjoyed my experiences in the field, I knew that I eventually wanted to work on data analytics, so I went back to school to learn technical data science skills such as programming, machine learning, spatial statistics, ecological forecasting, and more (as shown by my transcripts).
