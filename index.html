Career Summary

I earned my B.S. in Environmental Science and Terrestrial Resource Management from the University of Washington which is where I first learned about GIS. I took a class about spatial analysis in forest resources and afterwards, I immediately signed up as a GIS Lab Assistant to teach other students how to find spatial data and take advantage of this incredible technology. For my thesis, I used ArcGIS to classify aerial imagery by land cover type to assess habitat suitability for a native bee species. I worked a number of seasonal jobs that gave me experience with field research in the subject matters of plant-insect interactions, fisheries, disease vector control, and riparian conservation. While I enjoyed my experiences in the field, I knew that I eventually wanted to work on data analytics, so I went back to school to learn technical data science skills such as programming, machine learning, spatial statistics, ecological forecasting, and more (as shown by my transcript). My master's thesis (manuscript in preparation for submission to peer-reviewed journal) examine spatial and temporal variations in ecosystem resistance to seasonal drought. Now, I'm pursuing a career that will allow me to build upon my analytical skills while working with complex ecological data.
