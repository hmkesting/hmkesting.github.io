Modeling monthly precipitation isotope values with universal kriging

The goal of this project is to create monthly grids of precipitation isotope values covering the contiguous U.S. This is the first step of a larger project to analyze precipitation and runoff isotopes at sites across the country which ultimately addresses questions about ecosystem water use and vulnerability to seasonal drought. Here, we’ll just walk through the process of kriging January precipitation isotope values (δ2H). The raw data shown below were obtained from waterisotopes.org.



Moran’s I statistic showed the presence of spatial autocorrelation with a value of 0.34, meaning that similar values tend to be clustered. I conducted a variogram analysis. I manually limited the maximum range, then fit three variogram models: gaussian (blue), spherical (red), and exponential (yellow). In black, I also plotted the Monte Carlo envelope which shows the upper and lower bounds of a 95% confidence interval based on 1000 permutations of the data. The gaussian and spherical variogram models performed similarly well, but I moved forward with the gaussian model because it had a lower RMSE.



I kriged the values with elevation as a covariate, then plotted the 5-fold cross validation residuals spatially. There tended to be more negative residuals further north and more positive residuals further south, so I decided to include latitude as a covariate in the model.



Overall, I created 8 models and the one with the lowest AIC was an additive spatial model with covariates of elevation and latitude. Below is the result of kriging onto a spatial points grid.
